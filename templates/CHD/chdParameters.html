Parameters Related to CHD

  • Male:
    If Male than value is 1, if not than value is 0.
  • Age:
    The age of the person in years.
  • Education:
    Less than HSC level (1), HSC level (2), graduation (3) and post-graduation (4).
  • Current Smoker:
    If you are a current smoker than enter 1 otherwise 0.
  • Cigarettes Per Day:
    Number of cigarettes you consume in a day.
  • BP Medicines:
    If you are currently on blood pressure medicines than enter 1 otherwise 0.
  • Prevalent Stroke:
    If you previously had a stroke enter 1 otherwise 0.
  • Prevalent Hypertensive:
    If you were Hypertensive before enter 1 otherwise 0.
  • BP Medicines:
    If you are currently on blood pressure medicines than enter 1 otherwise 0.
  • Diabetes:
    If you are having diabetes enter 1 otherwise 0.
  • Total Cholesterol:
    High levels of total cholesterol, particularly LDL cholesterol, can lead to the buildup of plaque in the arteries, which can increase the risk of CHD.
  • Systolic Blood Pressure:
    Elevated systolic blood pressure, the pressure in the arteries when the heart beats, is a major risk factor for CHD. Over time, high blood pressure can damage the arteries and increase the risk of heart disease.
  • Diastolic Blood Pressure:
    Although less strongly linked to CHD than systolic blood pressure, high diastolic blood pressure, the pressure in the arteries when the heart is at rest, can also contribute to an increased risk of heart disease.
  • BMI:
    Body Mass Index, a measure of body fat based on height and weight.
  • Heart Rate:
    Resting heart rate is generally not considered a major risk factor for CHD, but higher heart rates during physical activity or exercise can indicate an increased risk of cardiovascular events. This may be due to increased strain on the heart or blood vessels, or because higher heart rates are often associated with other cardiovascular risk factors.
  • Glucose:
    The level of glucose (sugar) in the blood.
